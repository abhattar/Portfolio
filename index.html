Problem

The store and its websites are two points of contact for new customer, but neither is catered towards them. There are some misconceptions that exist among people who don't know much about homebrewing, which usually relates to investing a lot of time, space and money towards the hobby. In reality, it doesn't take up as much space, time or money as the users consider it does. User's are also not confident that they can follow up on the brewing process and it is some tedious highly focused task, which isn't really the case. The lack of visual appeal and organization, and the sense of being overwhelmed we re-affirms a users misconceptions could be why the store gets a fairly minimum engagement from new homebrewing enthusiasts. The ones who do come in, usually know a bit about homebrewing already through their social circle, mitigitaing their misconceptions.
